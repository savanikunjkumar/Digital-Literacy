# Stay Safe Online: Cybercrime Prevention Checklist

### 💳 Financial & UPI Safety
1. **Never share your UPI PIN to receive money:** A UPI PIN is *only* required when you are sending money or checking your balance. You never need a PIN to receive funds.
2. **Verify "Collect Requests" carefully:** Before approving any UPI mandate on Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm, double-check the sender's VPA ID and the exact amount being requested.
3. **Use secondary accounts for online transactions:** Link your primary UPI to a student bank account that only holds limited pocket money, keeping your main savings separate and secure.

### 🔒 General Online Safety
4. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Turn on 2FA for all critical accounts (university email, WhatsApp, GitHub) to block unauthorized access even if your password is leaked.
5. **Beware of urgent "part-time job" offers:** Be highly skeptical of WhatsApp or Telegram messages offering easy money for liking YouTube videos or leaving reviews; these are classic task-based scams.
6. **Keep your digital footprint clean:** Do not share sensitive documents (Aadhar card, PAN card, college ID) on public platforms or unverified group chats to prevent identity theft.
7. **Lock down social media privacy:** Set your personal accounts to private to prevent scammers from harvesting your photos or friend lists for social engineering.
8. **Think before you click:** Never click on suspicious SMS links claiming you have won a lottery, a free recharge, or warning that your bank account is blocked.

### 🚨 Emergency Reporting Contacts
*If you or a friend fall victim to a cybercrime, act immediately:*
* **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [www.cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
* **Cyber Crime Financial Fraud Helpline:** Dial **1930** (Call immediately to freeze fraudulent transactions).
