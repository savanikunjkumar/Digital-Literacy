# Case Study: UPI "Receive Money" Scam Targeting Students

**What is the crime?**
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a prevalent cybercrime where scammers manipulate individuals into authorizing malicious transactions. A widespread variation targeting students is the "QR Code/Receive Money" scam.

**How it typically happens:**
1. **Initial Contact:** A college student lists a used item (e.g., a textbook or laptop) for sale on an online marketplace or university WhatsApp group.
2. **The Hook:** The scammer poses as an eager buyer, agreeing to the asking price immediately to create a false sense of urgency.
3. **The Trap:** The scammer insists on paying via UPI. However, instead of sending money to the student's phone number, the scammer sends a QR code or a "Collect Request" notification.
4. **The Deception:** The scammer instructs the student to scan the code or approve the request and enter their UPI PIN, falsely claiming this is a necessary verification step to *receive* the funds.
5. **The Theft:** Since a UPI PIN is exclusively used to *send* money, once the student enters it, the funds are instantly deducted from their bank account and transferred to the scammer.

**Who is usually targeted?**
College students in India are prime targets because they frequently buy/sell used goods online, look for quick part-time jobs, and heavily rely on digital payment apps.

**Consequences:**
Victims suffer immediate financial loss, which can be severe for students on strict budgets. It also leads to emotional distress and a tedious recovery process involving banks and law enforcement.
