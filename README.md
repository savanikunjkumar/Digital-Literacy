## 📝 Task 1: Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic

**Tool Used:** Canva  
**Focus:** Digital Literacy Definition, Safe Internet Practices, and Essential Digital Tools.

**Project Report:**
For this task, I used Canva to design a one-page infographic titled "Empowering the Digital Student." I chose this tool for its intuitive drag-and-drop interface and professional templates, which allowed me to focus more on content hierarchy rather than basic layout.

My design covers three essential pillars: a clear definition of digital literacy, a checklist for safe internet practices (including 2FA and phishing awareness), and a curated list of tools like GitHub and LeetCode that are vital for modern computer science students. I used a clean, high-contrast color scheme to ensure the information is scannable and accessible for my batchmates.

One thing I found particularly interesting during this process was the challenge of "information distillation." It was difficult to take complex concepts like cybersecurity ethics and condense them into short, punchy bullet points without losing the core message. Balancing visual aesthetics with information density taught me that in digital communication, less is often more when trying to capture a student's attention.

### Part B: Social Media Do's and Don'ts Checklist

**✅ The 5 Do's**
1. **Do audit your digital footprint:** Regularly Google your own name to see what potential employers or professors might find.
2. **Do use platforms for networking:** Actively use LinkedIn to connect with university alumni, industry professionals, and recruiters.
3. **Do showcase your work:** Share your academic projects, GitHub repositories, and coding milestones to build a professional portfolio.
4. **Do utilize privacy settings:** Keep personal accounts private to separate your personal life from your professional image.
5. **Do engage respectfully:** Treat online discussions with the same professionalism you would use in a physical classroom or office.

**❌ The 5 Don'ts**
1. **Don't post illegal or inappropriate content:** Never share photos or posts involving underage drinking, drug use, or discriminatory language.
2. **Don't engage in public arguments:** Avoid getting dragged into heated, emotional debates in comment sections; it leaves a permanent negative footprint.
3. **Don't overshare personal information:** Never post your exact location, address, or sensitive university credentials online.
4. **Don't badmouth peers or professors:** Venting about university staff or classmates online is highly unprofessional and can lead to disciplinary action.
5. **Don't forget that the internet is forever:** Never assume a disappearing story or a deleted tweet is truly gone; screenshots exist permanently.
